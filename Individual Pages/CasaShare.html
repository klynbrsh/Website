CasaShare

Through extensive statistical analysis and in-depth interviews, my team and I developed an app that would allow roommates and landlords to communicate and pay bills in a more effective way. We designed the platform so that users could pay bills individually, which would allow other users sharing space with them to avoid any confrontation that might come from missing payments or other bill-sharing conflicts. Although making bill- paying easier was our primary goal, we also incorporated making listing properties easier for the first-time landlords.

User Research
Defining the user

This project was on a time frame of 3 weeks and had to include a working coded prototype. I decided to keep us to a time frame of one week for each part of the process (research, design, coding). Because of this, we only had time to do a couple of interviews and some demographic research. We researched quantitative data on renting in the US. We learned how many people rent/ rent out their homes as well as some of the reasons why people rent. Through our interviews, we learned about problems faced by renters in the Austin area.

Persona and Journey Map

There are 2 aspects of renting (the homeowners/ the renters). We decided to focus on the property holder and created a homeowner user persona, Malik. This was done not only to incorporate all of our research into a more empathetic user but also to provide the team with a way to prevent scope creep from setting in. Using our persona, we created a journey map to help us determine the main problems homeowners face when they decide to rent out rooms. We based this map on Malik’s journey trying to navigate all of this for the first time on his own without our app.

Finding Solution

While creating our persona and journey map, we had multiple discussions on where this app could go and what exactly we should focus on. We started this process with the idea that the app would allow renters to make loans to each other to help cover late rent payments. We then played with the idea of allowing each user to pay their portions directly rather than paying one person who paid the owner. After finishing our persona and journey map we realized that none of these issues are a focus for the homeowner. In the end, we decided to create an app to allow Malik to easily post his home on the market, send out the rules with confirmation they have been read, and create a system for what happens if the renter misses a payment.

Sketches

We started with some creating two sets of sketches. After testing them both, we moved on to creating whiteboard sketches that combined the best parts of each set. After testing the whiteboard sketches with some of our interviewees, we moved into creating low-res wireframes. We decided to stick to a simple flow, with the main emphasis on Malik adding his home to the renter market. Through our research, we learned that most people aren’t aware of what all they should or should not post, where they should post it, or the legal stuff behind posting their house on the market.

Low, High and Color

Focusing on the above tasks, we were able to simplify the screens into a very simple process. We kept our design clean and easy to use, as dealing with all of these issues is very stressful for first time home renters. We chose our colors based on color psychology because we wanted something calming but exciting for users that also conveyed trustworthiness and positivity. We created the high-res wireframes to incorporate these colors without overwhelming the users, while also maintaining a clean and professional feel.

Deliverables

This was a boot camp project, so I wanted to give all the members in the group an opportunity to code out a page of the site. This allowed them to learn how to stick closely to the wireframe as well as the specifications set out beforehand. At the end of the project, I set to work pulling all of the pages together and I presented our final project.

Final Thoughts

Leading a 6-person team (full of strong personalities) through the entire UX process was a big challenge for me. Being in a boot camp setting, I struggled with giving everyone a chance to work on every aspect of the process while also creating the best product we could. Going forward with this assignment, I would have liked to focus more on making the prototype look a little more professional, but overall I am very happy with the way things turned out.
