Overview

A redesign of the Amazon Website to make it more intuitive for users. The four member team also worked to unify the design across all devices.


Over the course of three weeks my group and I interviewed about 25 individuals. Our research revealed a few pain points that a new feature might help eliminate. We redesigned the platform so that users could exercise these features (individualizing their recommendations) to help Amazon recommend more relatable products in the hopes it would increase sales. Although individualizing was the main goal, in our Discovery phase we worked on the idea of minimizing the amount of space it took to display information and decided to follow both directions.

**If you are more interested in the visual work of this project, feel free to skip to the Development phase.**

Discover

While the brief was fairly open-ended, my team and I were interested in the overall problems faced in using Amazon Prime and their Desktop site. All of us experienced friction trying to find the cheapest, highest quality product that best fit what we wanted. Our first round of interviews set out to discover how other people interact with Amazon in their daily lives.

Survey Results

More than 70% of our interviewees answered that they start on Amazon with a product already in mind and the search bar is their first click. They also indicated that a lot of the time they use reviews as their only means of quality checking before purchasing an item. In the next set of interviews we honed in on this behavior. We also looked into the other aspects of Amazon we were still debating on including (gift-recommendations and deal-notifications).




SWOT Analysis


Attempts to research how to implement a gift-recommendation feature or a deal-notification system led to the discovery that Amazon already has these features incorporated. After some testing to determine how many people knew about these functions, we determined that Amazon had to many features hidden in places most users didnâ€™t know to look.





In order to keep to the time frame, we broke down the discovery results into several areas we felt that with a little more research could become full projects. We broke the project down into four categories of interest. We then did more interviewing and surveying around these categories:

  • Streamlining through overcrowding pages

  • Price/Deal notifications

  • Customizing home page

  • Filtering- change location and hierarchy

Define

A decent way into the project timeline, my team and I were concerned. We discovered multiple pain points for users but still had no real clear sense of hierarchy between them and no idea on which point to focus on and how to go about fixing it.



Problem Areas


Minimalism: simplifying and changing heirarchy

Amazon has a lot going for it. Millions of items, that ship to millions of people, across multiple platforms. They have hundreds of features and several options when it comes to searching for that perfect item and filtering through those results. The need for simplification was obvious, but what information did users want to see and what was the best way to convey it.


Customization of Home Page: a personal approach

People love personalized things. Why should product recommendations be any different? Not only would personalizing recommendations benefit the user, it would also benefit Amazon. By increasing interest in related products, Amazon might experience more sales and higher customer interaction.

Price/Deal Notifications: Is cheaper always better

It was common for our interviewees to use multiple e-commerce sites to find the best deals. But it was easy to imagine scenarios in which a user might subscribe to just one because of the great deals and prices Amazon offers. The need for a better tool to notify users of the best deals and prices across all of their interests and products appeared more pronounced.
