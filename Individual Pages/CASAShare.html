Overview

For this project, my team and I were handed the broad category - "Real estate" and challenged to develop a mobile app. Through extensive statistical analysis and in-depth interviews, we came up with an app that would allow roommates and landlords to communicate and pay bills in a more effective way. Over the course of three weeks, my group and I designed the platform so that users could pay bills individually, which would allow other users sharing space with them to avoid any confrontation that might come from missing payments or other bill sharing conflicts. Although making bill paying easier was the main goal, in our Discovery phase, we worked on the idea of making listing properties easier for the first-time listers.

Team Lead: Kailyn Brush

Group Members: Jeffrey Aselu, Andrew Brouillard, Mike Carreon, Marisa Cullen, Darshpreet Kaur

Discover

The brief was fairly open-ended, so my team and I chose to focus on the overall problems facing a majority of people who own/rent space. All the team members have experienced living with roommates and having to deal with sharing rent money and/or missing payments, which is why this was our first area to explore.

Our first round of interviews and research set out to discover how other people interact with their roommates and how they pay their bills. We based our interview questions on a general assumption that many people had undesireable experiences when it came to missing payments, awkward confrontations, and annoying Venmo transfers. The interviews confirmed that a lot of users experienced these issues so we dove into the best way to combine all these concerns and solve them with a single solution.



Define

The team decided to simplify the pain points for users and the next steps were to determine the hierarchy between the pain points and how to go about solving the chosen problem areas.



Problem Areas


Paying someone else to pay your bills is complicated and not always safe.

When it came to paying bills, all the people we interviewed said they pay one person through Venmo who then pays the bills. Approximately 35% of the users said they aren't always sure the roommate actually pays the bills and they don't really know where their money is going. A majority of the users also said that missing payments caused the conversations and the entire household environment to get really intense, awkward, and/or uncomfortable.


Listing a home is complicated and full of legal jargon.

Often the people we interviewed that had listed their homes or thought about doing so said it was a very complicated and overwhelming process. Many of them were unable to list their property in as desirable manner as they had hoped and had either found not-so-great tenants or were put off entirely by the process and gave up.



Character Profiles


To match our interviews/survey results, we created a character profile for Malik Simon. Malik is a first-time homeowner and is renting a space to an old friend he doesn't really know anymore. Malik doesn't have a lot of time to focus on listing his room, but he is really excited to live with his old friend and make some extra cash.

We settled on a combination of the color schemes for two reasons. First, both lighter colors were aesthetically pleasing and had the right effects psychologically that we wanted to convey.
