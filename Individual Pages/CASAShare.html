Overview

For this project my team and I were handed the category of real estate and told to develop a mobile app. Through a lot of statistical analysis and interviews we came up with an app that would allow roommates and landlords to communicate and pay bills in a more effective way. Over the course of three weeks, my group and I interviewed approximately 15 individuals. We designed the platform so that users could pay bills individually, which would allow other users sharing space with them to avoid that confrontation. Although making bill payments easier was the main goal, in our Discovery phase we worked on the idea of making listing properties easier for the first time listers.

Team Lead: Kailyn Brush

Group Members: Jeffrey Aselu, Andrew Brouillard, Mike Carreon, Marisa Cullen, Darshpreet Kaur

Discover

While the brief was fairly open-ended, my team and I were interested in the overall problems facing a majority of people who own/rent space. All of the team members have experienced living with roommates and having to deal with sharing rent money, or missing payments, which is why this was our first area to explore. Our first round of interviews and research set out to discover how other people interact with thier roommates and how they pay their bills. We came up with our interview questions based on a general overview of the direction we thought we wanted to head in. Based on that we had a few assumptions that people had bad experiences when it came to missing payments, awkward confrontations, and annoying venmo transfers. Through the interviews we learned that a lot of these were experienced so we dove into the best way to combine all of these problems and solve them with a single solution.





Define

The team decided to simplify the pain points for users and the next steps were to determine the hierarchy between them and how to go about solving the chosen problem.



Problem Areas


Paying someone else to pay your bills is complicated and not always safe.

When it came to paying bills, all the people we interviewed said they pay one person through venmo who then pays the bills. About 35% of them said they aren't always sure the roommate pays the bills and they don't really know where their money is going. A majority of them also said when it came to missing a payment the conversation and entire household would get really intense, awkward or uncomfortable.


Listing a home was complicated and full of legal jargon.

For the people we interviewed that had listed their homes or thought about it a lot of them said it was very complicated and overwhelming. Many of them weren't able to list their property as well as they had wanted to and had either recieved not so great tenants or were put off entirely and gave up.



Character Profiles


To match our interviews/survey results, we created a character profile for Malik Simon. Malik is a first time homeowner and is renting a space to an old friend he doesn't really know anymore. Malik doens't have a lot of time to focus on listing his room but he is really excited to live with his old friend and make some extra cash.

A&B Testing

For the A&B testing we used the paper wireframes we had created and made a Invision prototype for users to test. They were very candid about their concerns: a distaste for the reviews and product description layout and a general dislike of the onboarding process led to a complete redesign of both. Several users also made it clear that the icons were a little hard to understand which led to a change of design for those as well.


"I actually go through the negative reviews, not the positive ones. But that's really the first thing I look at."

"I actually like the different icons (wishlist, and add to cart icons). It's pretty slick looking!"

"I generally prefer very minimal [menus]. I have no idea why...It just seems natural."
