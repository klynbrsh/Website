Lombardo Law Firm

Over the course of three weeks, I tested the layouts and experience of two different versions of the Lombardo law site in California. In the discovery phase, I uncovered issues with site navigation and decided to focus on both layout and navigation. I conducted my research to determine the best way to provide potential users with an easier navigation system and more pleasing design system.

User Research
Defining the user

Because this project was done remotely in a different state, I had to do some digging to determine the user of this website. I conducted demographic research as well as conducting stakeholder interviews to get their business statistics. The main user turned out to be white males with an income of 100k - 150k.

A&B Testing

The stakeholders wanted to focus primarily on which site was better. In doing so, I decided to focus on A/B testing. I conducted 5 interviews, local to Austin, to determine the main pain points before moving to interviewing users local to California. The main pain points showed that neither site was very good. One site had a bad navigation experience while the other had a bad design experience. I passed off my findings to the design team and recommended finding a way to combine the two.

Accessibility Testing

Testing for accessibility was a new challenge for me. Due to the site being an injury law firm I knew that accessibility would be important. I conducted color contrast tests as well as screen reader tests. Going through the site using the keyboard was also important and I found many discrepancies. Accessibility was a huge problem on both sites and needed to be handled better in the future design.

Heuristic Evaluation

The client wanted to determine which site appealed to users and if there was a way to make it better. Through surveys, interviews, and heuristic evaluations, I found several areas that needed improvement.
